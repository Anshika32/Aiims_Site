Women's Health

Breast cancer is one of the most common cancers among women in the reproductive age group. With an estimated 2.3 million cases every year, approximately 1.15 million people globally are diagnosed with breast cancer. The global burden of breast cancer is projected to surpass 2 million cases by 2030.

In India, as of 2018, breast cancer accounted for 32.8% of all cancers in women aged 25-49, with a mortality rate of 27.7%. Early detection through Breast Self-Examination (BSE) is crucial. BSE is a simple, cost-effective, and non-invasive method recommended monthly after the menstrual period.

About Breast Cancer

What is Breast Cancer?

Breast cancer is a disease where breast cells grow uncontrollably. It can develop in different parts of the breast and, if detected early, can be treated effectively.

Common signs include lumps in the breast, changes in size/shape, and unusual pain. Regular screenings and self-examinations aid early detection.

Why is Early Detection Important?

Early detection greatly improves survival rates. Methods such as Breast Self-Examination (BSE), clinical exams, and mammograms help identify cancer at an early stage.

Studies show that early detection increases the chances of successful treatment and reduces mortality rates significantly.

How to Perform Breast Self-Examination (BSE)?

BSE is a simple method done at home every month to check for abnormalities in the breast:

  • Stand in front of a mirror and inspect for changes in shape, size, or color.
  • Raise your arms and check for dimpling, puckering, or swelling.
  • Use your fingertips to feel for lumps in circular motions across the breast.
  • Repeat the process while lying down for a better examination.
